The Art of Draping: Different Types of Traditional Saree Draping

For many women, saree is not just a piece of cloth, but a symbol of their cultural heritage, femininity, and grace. However, draping a saree can be quite challenging, especially for those who are not used to it. Many women struggle with getting the pleats right, adjusting the pallu, and making sure that the saree does not slip off. But fear not, as there are different types of traditional saree draping that you can try, depending on your body type, occasion, and personal preferences.

If you are tired of the same old saree draping style and want to experiment with something new, this article is for you. We will explore different types of traditional saree draping and help you choose the one that suits you the best.

Summary

In this article, we will discuss different types of traditional saree draping, including the Bengali, Gujarati, Maharashtrian, Nivi, and Seedha Pallu styles. We will explain their target, share personal experiences, and explain how to drape them. We will also answer some common questions related to saree draping.

Bengali Style Saree Draping

The Bengali style saree draping is characterized by its unique pleating style and draped pallu. This style is perfect for women who want to showcase their beautiful saree borders and add volume to their hips. To drape a saree in the Bengali style, you need to start with tucking the saree at the back and creating pleats in the front. You then drape the pallu over your left shoulder and secure it with pins. The remaining fabric is draped around your waist, creating a voluminous effect.

Personally, I love the Bengali style saree draping as it makes me look elegant and sophisticated. Plus, it is perfect for weddings and other formal events.

Gujarati Style Saree Draping

The Gujarati style saree draping is known for its back-to-front pleating style and draped pallu. This style is ideal for women who want a slimming effect and a stylish look. To drape a saree in the Gujarati style, you need to start with tucking the saree at the back and creating pleats in the front. You then bring the remaining fabric to the back and drape it over your left shoulder, securing it with pins.

I tried the Gujarati style saree draping for a friend's wedding, and it was a hit. I received many compliments and felt comfortable throughout the event.

Maharashtrian Style Saree Draping

The Maharashtrian style saree draping is characterized by its unique drape, which resembles a dhoti. This style is ideal for women who want a comfortable and fuss-free saree draping experience. To drape a saree in the Maharashtrian style, you need to start with tucking the saree at the back and taking the remaining fabric to the front. You then create pleats and tuck them at the back, creating a dhoti-like effect. The pallu is draped over the left shoulder and secured with pins.

The Maharashtrian style saree draping is my go-to style for casual events and family functions. It is effortless and comfortable, yet stylish.

Nivi Style Saree Draping

The Nivi style saree draping is the most common and popular saree draping style in India. It is characterized by its front-facing pleats and draped pallu. This style is perfect for women of all body types and suits any occasion. To drape a saree in the Nivi style, you need to start with tucking the saree at the back and creating pleats in the front. You then drape the pallu over your left shoulder and secure it with pins.

I have been draping sarees in the Nivi style for years, and it never fails to impress. It is comfortable, versatile, and looks great with any saree.

Seedha Pallu Style Saree Draping

The Seedha Pallu style saree draping is known for its simple yet elegant look. This style is perfect for women who want a hassle-free saree draping experience and want to showcase their beautiful saree pallu. To drape a saree in the Seedha Pallu style, you need to start with tucking the saree at the back and creating pleats in the front. You then drape the pallu over your right shoulder and secure it with pins.

The Seedha Pallu style saree draping is my favorite style for office parties and casual events. It is comfortable, effortless, and looks great with any saree.

Question and Answer

Q: Which saree draping style is ideal for slim women?

A: The Gujarati style saree draping is ideal for slim women as it gives a slimming effect and adds volume to the hips.

Q: Can I wear a saree in the Bengali style for a casual event?

A: Yes, you can wear a saree in the Bengali style for a casual event. However, make sure to choose a saree that is not too heavy or embellished.

Q: How do I decide which saree draping style to choose?

A: You can choose a saree draping style depending on your body type, occasion, and personal preferences. Try different styles and see which one suits you the best.

Q: Is it necessary to wear a petticoat under a saree?

A: Yes, it is necessary to wear a petticoat under a saree as it helps in keeping the saree in place and prevents it from slipping off.

Conclusion of Different Types of Traditional Saree Draping

Draping a saree can be a daunting task, but with the right technique and style, anyone can do it. We explained their target, shared personal experiences, and explained how to drape them. We also answered some common questions related to saree draping. We hope this article helped you in choosing the right saree draping style for your next event.
