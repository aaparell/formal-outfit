The "Thrilla in Manila"

One of the most famous boxing fights of all time, the "Thrilla in Manila" was the third and final fight between Muhammad Ali and Joe Frazier in 1975. The fight was held in the Philippines and was watched by millions of people around the world. It was an intense and grueling battle that lasted 14 rounds and ended with Ali winning by TKO. The fight is widely regarded as one of the greatest in boxing history and is a must-watch for any boxing fan.

The "Rumble in the Jungle"

The "Rumble in the Jungle" is another iconic boxing fight that took place in 1974 between Muhammad Ali and George Foreman. The fight was held in Kinshasa, Zaire, and was watched by an estimated one billion people worldwide. Ali was the underdog going into the fight, but he used his "rope-a-dope" technique to tire out Foreman and eventually knock him out in the eighth round. The "Rumble in the Jungle" is a classic example of an underdog coming out on top and is a must-watch for any boxing fan.

The "Fight of the Century"

The "Fight of the Century" was the first fight between Muhammad Ali and Joe Frazier in 1971. The fight was held at Madison Square Garden in New York City and was watched by millions of people around the world. The fight was a highly anticipated showdown between two undefeated heavyweight champions, and it lived up to its billing. Frazier won the fight by unanimous decision, but the "Fight of the Century" is still regarded as one of the greatest fights in boxing history.

The "Golden Boy" vs. "Pretty Boy"

The fight between Oscar De La Hoya and Floyd Mayweather Jr. in 2007 was highly anticipated, as it pitted two of the best boxers of their generation against each other. De La Hoya was the "Golden Boy" of boxing, while Mayweather was known as "Pretty Boy." The fight was a back-and-forth battle that went the distance, with Mayweather winning by split decision. The fight is a classic example of two great boxers going head-to-head and is a must-watch for any boxing fan.
