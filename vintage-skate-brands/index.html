The Ultimate Guide To Vintage Skate Brands

Written by Bardi Sep 29, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you a fan of vintage skate brands but don't know where to start? Do you want to know more about the history and culture behind these iconic brands? Look no further, as we've got you covered!

Vintage skate brands have been around since the birth of skateboarding in the 1950s. These brands reflect the rebellious and countercultural spirit of skateboarding, and have become an integral part of the skateboarding community.

In this guide, we'll cover everything you need to know about vintage skate brands, from their history and culture to the most popular brands of all time.

Vision Street Wear

One of the most popular vintage skate brands of all time is Vision Street Wear. Founded in the early 1980s, Vision Street Wear was known for its bold graphics and innovative designs. Their shoes, in particular, were a favorite among skaters due to their durability and style.

As a personal experience, I remember wearing my first pair of Vision Street Wear shoes as a teenager and feeling like the coolest person in the world. The brand was ubiquitous in skate culture, and their influence can still be seen in modern skate fashion today.

Powell Peralta

Powell Peralta is another iconic vintage skate brand that has stood the test of time. Founded in the late 1970s, Powell Peralta was known for its innovative skateboard designs and team of legendary skaters, including Tony Hawk and Steve Caballero.

As a personal experience, I remember watching videos of Tony Hawk and other Powell Peralta team riders performing incredible tricks and being awestruck. Powell Peralta was a symbol of the creativity and athleticism of skateboarding, and their impact on the sport and culture cannot be overstated.

The Culture of Vintage Skate Brands

One of the defining features of vintage skate brands is their association with skate culture. Skateboarding has always been a countercultural activity, and vintage skate brands reflect this rebellious spirit.

Skateboarding is also an art form, and vintage skate brands have always been at the forefront of skateboard design and innovation. From the shape of the board to the graphics on the deck, vintage skate brands have pushed the boundaries of what is possible in skateboarding.

How to Start Collecting Vintage Skate Brands

If you're interested in collecting vintage skate brands, there are a few things you should keep in mind. First, do your research and learn about the history and culture of skateboarding and vintage skate brands. This will give you a better understanding of what to look for and how to evaluate the value of different brands and products.

Second, start small and build your collection gradually. Look for vintage skate brands at flea markets, garage sales, and online marketplaces. Don't be afraid to ask questions and negotiate prices, as many sellers may not be aware of the true value of the items they are selling.

Dogtown Skateboards

Dogtown Skateboards is another vintage skate brand that has become a cultural icon. Founded in the mid-1970s, Dogtown Skateboards was known for its team of legendary skaters, including Tony Alva and Stacy Peralta.

As a personal experience, I remember watching videos of the Dogtown team and being blown away by their raw skill and creativity. Dogtown Skateboards represented the rebellious spirit of skateboarding, and their influence can still be felt in modern skate culture.

Question and Answer

Q: What makes vintage skate brands so popular?

A: Vintage skate brands are popular because they reflect the rebellious and countercultural spirit of skateboarding. They are also associated with the creativity and athleticism of skateboarding, which has made them an integral part of skate culture.

Q: How can I tell if a vintage skate brand is authentic?

A: The best way to tell if a vintage skate brand is authentic is to do your research and learn about the history and culture of the brand. Look for identifying marks and logos, and compare the item to known examples of authentic products from the brand.

Q: What are some of the most valuable vintage skate brands?

A: Some of the most valuable vintage skate brands include Powell Peralta, Vision Street Wear, and Dogtown Skateboards. These brands have become cultural icons and are highly sought after by collectors.

Q: Can I still buy vintage skate brands today?

A: Yes, many vintage skate brands are still available today, either as reissues or new products. However, these items may be more expensive than vintage items, so it's important to do your research and shop around to find the best deals.

Conclusion of Vintage Skate Brands

Vintage skate brands are an integral part of skate culture and have become cultural icons in their own right. From Powell Peralta to Vision Street Wear, these brands reflect the rebellious and countercultural spirit of skateboarding, and their influence can still be seen in modern skate culture today. Whether you're a collector or just a fan of skateboarding, vintage skate brands are a must-know topic.
