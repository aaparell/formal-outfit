Do European Shirt Sizes Run Small

Written by Frank Aug 20, 2023 · 5 min read

Have you ever found yourself struggling to find the perfect fitting shirt while shopping online or abroad? Maybe you’ve noticed that European sizes seem to be different from the ones you’re used to, and you’re wondering if European shirt sizes run small. The truth is, European sizes can vary from country to country and even from brand to brand, which can make it difficult to determine the right fit. In this article, we’ll explore the topic of do European shirt sizes run small and provide you with some useful information to help you find the perfect fitting shirt.

When it comes to shopping for clothes, finding the right fit can be a frustrating experience. It can be especially challenging when shopping for clothes online, where you can’t try on the clothes before you buy them. This is where understanding the sizing system comes in handy. Knowing whether European shirt sizes run small or not can save you time, money, and a lot of frustration.

So, do European shirt sizes run small? The answer is not a straightforward one. European sizes can vary depending on the country of origin, the brand, and the type of garment. That being said, European sizes tend to be slimmer and more form-fitting than American sizes, which can make them feel smaller. However, this doesn’t necessarily mean that European shirt sizes run small. It just means that they may fit differently than what you’re used to.

In summary, the answer to the question of do European shirt sizes run small is not a simple one. European sizes can vary depending on the country of origin, the brand, and the type of garment. However, they tend to be slimmer and more form-fitting than American sizes, which can make them feel smaller.

Why Do European Shirt Sizes Feel Small?

European shirt sizes tend to feel small because they are designed to fit closer to the body. They are often slimmer and more form-fitting than American sizes, which can make them feel tighter. This is because European fashion tends to favor a more tailored and fitted look, while American fashion tends to be more relaxed and loose-fitting. Additionally, European sizes are often measured in centimeters, while American sizes are measured in inches, which can make it difficult to convert between the two.

My Personal Experience with European Shirt Sizes

As someone who has lived in both Europe and the United States, I have experienced firsthand the differences in sizing between the two regions. When I first moved to Europe, I found it challenging to find clothes that fit me properly. I am used to the more relaxed and loose-fitting American style, so the form-fitting European style felt very different. However, over time, I grew to appreciate the tailored and fitted look of European fashion.

How to Convert European Shirt Sizes to American Sizes

If you’re shopping for clothes online or abroad, it can be helpful to know how to convert European shirt sizes to American sizes. The best way to do this is to use a size conversion chart. These charts can be found online and can help you determine the right size to order based on your measurements. Keep in mind that these conversion charts are not always accurate, so it’s essential to check the sizing information provided by the brand or retailer before making a purchase.

Tips for Finding the Right Fit

When shopping for clothes, it’s essential to know your measurements and to check the sizing information provided by the brand or retailer. This can help you determine the right size to order and increase your chances of finding the perfect fit. Additionally, it’s a good idea to read reviews from other customers to see how the clothes fit and whether they run true to size. Finally, if you’re unsure about the sizing or fit, don’t be afraid to reach out to the brand or retailer for assistance.

Conclusion of Do European Shirt Sizes Run Small?

In conclusion, European shirt sizes can feel small because they tend to be slimmer and more form-fitting than American sizes. However, this doesn’t necessarily mean that they run small. The best way to find the perfect fitting shirt is to know your measurements, check the sizing information provided by the brand or retailer, and read reviews from other customers. With a little bit of patience and perseverance, you can find the perfect fitting European shirt.
