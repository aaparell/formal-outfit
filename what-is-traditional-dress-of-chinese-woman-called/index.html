So, what is traditional dress of Chinese women called? The traditional dress for women in China is called a qipao, also known as a cheongsam. It is a form-fitting dress that features a high collar, short sleeves, and a slit on the side of the skirt. The dress is typically made from silk or other luxurious fabrics and is adorned with intricate embroidery, beading, or other embellishments.

Now that we know what is traditional dress of Chinese women called, let's dive deeper into its history and significance. The qipao has a long and rich history that dates back to the Qing Dynasty (1644-1912). It was originally worn by Manchu women and was a loose-fitting dress that covered the entire body. However, over time, the dress evolved to become more form-fitting and revealing. Today, the qipao is considered a symbol of Chinese culture and is often worn during special occasions, such as weddings, Chinese New Year, and other celebrations.
