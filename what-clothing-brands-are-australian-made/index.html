Discovering What Clothing Brands Are Australian Made

Written by Bardi Jul 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you want to support Australian businesses and the local economy by purchasing clothing made in Australia? Are you unsure which clothing brands are Australian made? You're not alone. Many consumers are conscious of the impact of their purchases and want to support local industries. In this blog post, we'll explore what clothing brands are Australian made, their benefits, and how to find them.

It can be frustrating to want to support Australian-made but not know which brands to look for. Many consumers are also concerned about the quality of Australian-made clothing and whether it's worth the extra cost. Additionally, some consumers may not be aware of the environmental and ethical benefits of purchasing Australian-made clothing. These are all valid concerns that we'll address in this post.

The good news is that there are many Australian-made clothing brands, ranging from small boutique labels to larger, well-known brands. These brands offer a range of clothing, including casual wear, activewear, and formal wear. Some popular Australian-made clothing brands include Country Road, Cue, and RM Williams. You can also find Australian-made clothing at specialty stores such as The Fable and Citizen Wolf.

In summary, purchasing Australian-made clothing supports local businesses and the economy, benefits the environment and workers, and can offer high-quality and unique clothing options. To find Australian-made clothing, look for the 'Australian Made' logo or search for brands that are transparent about their production methods and materials.

Finding Australian-Made Clothing Brands Online

When shopping online, it can be challenging to know which brands are Australian-made. However, there are several ways to find these brands. One option is to use online directories such as The Australian Made Campaign or Ethical Clothing Australia. These directories list Australian-made clothing brands and provide information about the materials and production processes used.

Another way to find Australian-made clothing brands online is to search for them using specific keywords. Try searching for 'Australian-made clothing brands' or 'ethical Australian clothing brands' to find relevant results. You can also explore online marketplaces such as Etsy, which features many Australian-made clothing brands.

The Benefits of Purchasing Australian-Made Clothing Brands

Purchasing Australian-made clothing brands offers several benefits, including supporting the local economy, promoting sustainable and ethical practices, and offering high-quality and unique clothing options.

When you purchase Australian-made clothing, you're supporting local businesses and the economy. This helps create jobs and supports the growth and development of Australian industries. Additionally, purchasing Australian-made clothing promotes sustainable and ethical practices, such as using eco-friendly materials and ensuring fair labor practices.

Australian-made clothing brands also offer high-quality and unique clothing options. Many brands use high-quality materials and pay attention to the details of their designs, resulting in clothing that lasts longer and looks better than fast fashion alternatives.

How to Identify Australian-Made Clothing

The easiest way to identify Australian-made clothing is to look for the 'Australian Made' logo. This logo is a registered trademark that can only be used by companies that meet specific Australian-made criteria. These criteria include that the product is substantially transformed in Australia, at least 50% of the cost of production was incurred in Australia, and the product is of a high standard.

You can also look for brands that are transparent about their production methods and materials. Many Australian-made clothing brands will include information on their website about where and how their clothing is made, the materials used, and any ethical and sustainable practices they follow.

Where to Find Australian-Made Clothing Brands In-Store

If you prefer to shop in-store, there are several places you can find Australian-made clothing brands. Many larger department stores such as David Jones and Myer stock Australian-made clothing brands such as Sportscraft and SABA. Additionally, boutique stores often stock Australian-made clothing brands, and you can find these stores in many shopping districts.

Conclusion of What Clothing Brands Are Australian Made

Supporting Australian-made clothing brands is a great way to support the local economy, promote ethical and sustainable practices, and find unique and high-quality clothing options. To find Australian-made clothing, look for the 'Australian Made' logo or search for brands that are transparent about their production methods and materials. Whether you prefer to shop online or in-store, there are plenty of Australian-made clothing options available.
