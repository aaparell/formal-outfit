What are Men's Muslim Robes Called and What Do They Represent?

Men's Muslim robes are called different things depending on the region and the style of the garment. However, the most common term used to refer to these garments in English is "thobe" or "thawb." These garments are typically long, loose-fitting robes that are worn over other clothing, and they come in a variety of colors and styles.

The thobe is a symbol of modesty and respect in Islamic culture. It's worn by men of all ages, and it's often worn during religious occasions and ceremonies. The thobe is also a practical garment that can help keep the wearer cool in hot climates.

The Different Styles of Men's Muslim Robes

There are many different styles of men's Muslim robes, and each one has its own unique characteristics. Some of the most common styles include:

The Dishdasha

The dishdasha is a long, flowing robe that is typically worn in the Gulf region. It's made from lightweight fabrics, and it's often worn in white or light colors to reflect the harsh sunlight.

The Jubba

The jubba is a long robe that is commonly worn in North Africa and the Middle East. It's often made from heavier fabrics and can be worn in a variety of colors and patterns.

The Kurta

The kurta is a shorter, more fitted robe that is typically worn in South Asia. It's often worn with loose-fitting pants and can be made from a variety of fabrics.

The Thawb

The thawb is a long, flowy robe that is commonly worn in the Levant region. It's often worn in white or light colors and can be made from lightweight fabrics.

Choose the Right Fabric

Pair it with the Right Shoes

Accessorize with a Kufi

A kufi is a traditional Muslim hat that can be worn with a thobe. It's often made from cotton or silk and comes in a variety of colors.
