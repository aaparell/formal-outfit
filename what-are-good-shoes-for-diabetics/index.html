What Are Good Shoes For Diabetics

Written by Bardi Jun 24, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Living with diabetes can be challenging, especially when it comes to footwear. Many diabetics experience foot problems such as poor circulation, neuropathy, and foot ulcers. These issues can be exacerbated by ill-fitting or uncomfortable shoes. Finding the right shoes is crucial for diabetics to prevent further foot complications.

So, what are good shoes for diabetics? Shoes that provide support, cushioning, and a proper fit are essential. These shoes should also have a wide toe box, a non-slip sole, and be made from breathable materials. Additionally, shoes that are adjustable, such as those with laces or straps, are recommended as they can accommodate swelling or changes in foot size.

In summary, good shoes for diabetics should have the following features:

  • Support
  • Cushioning
  • Proper fit
  • Wide toe box
  • Non-slip sole
  • Breathable materials
  • Adjustable

My Personal Experience

As a diabetic myself, finding comfortable and supportive shoes has always been a challenge. I used to wear shoes that were too tight, which caused blisters and calluses on my feet. After consulting with my doctor, I switched to shoes that had a wider toe box and better cushioning. The difference was amazing. My feet were less sore, and I had fewer bouts of swelling and neuropathy.

Types of Good Shoes for Diabetics

There are several types of shoes that are specifically designed for diabetics. Some of these include:

  • Orthopedic shoes: These shoes are designed to provide support and cushioning for those with foot problems. They often have a removable insole, which can be replaced with custom orthotics.
  • Diabetic shoes: These shoes are made with breathable materials and have a wide toe box to prevent pressure on the toes. They also have a non-slip sole and are adjustable to accommodate swelling or changes in foot size.
  • Custom shoes: For those with severe foot problems, custom shoes may be necessary. These shoes are made to fit the individual's foot shape and provide the necessary support and cushioning.

Benefits of Good Shoes for Diabetics

Wearing good shoes can have numerous benefits for diabetics, including:

  • Preventing foot ulcers and other foot complications
  • Reducing foot pain and discomfort
  • Improving circulation in the feet
  • Preventing falls and slips
  • Improving overall foot health

Tips for Choosing Good Shoes for Diabetics

When shopping for shoes, keep the following tips in mind:

  • Get your feet measured regularly to ensure a proper fit.
  • Look for shoes with a wide toe box and breathable materials.
  • Choose shoes with a non-slip sole to prevent falls.
  • Consider shoes that are adjustable to accommodate swelling or changes in foot size.
  • Consult with a podiatrist or diabetes specialist for recommendations on specific shoe brands.

FAQs

Q: Can I wear sandals or flip-flops as a diabetic?

A: It is not recommended to wear sandals or flip-flops as they do not provide adequate support or cushioning for the feet. Additionally, they leave the feet exposed to potential injury.

Q: Can I wear high heels as a diabetic?

A: High heels are not recommended as they can cause pressure points and lead to foot problems such as blisters, calluses, and even ulcers.

Q: Are there specific shoe brands that are recommended for diabetics?

A: There are several shoe brands that are designed specifically for diabetics, including Dr. Comfort, Orthofeet, and New Balance.

Q: Can I wear athletic shoes as a diabetic?

A: Athletic shoes can be a good choice for diabetics as they often provide support, cushioning, and a proper fit. Look for shoes that have a non-slip sole and are made from breathable materials.

Conclusion

Good shoes for diabetics are essential for preventing foot complications and improving overall foot health. When choosing shoes, look for ones that have support, cushioning, a proper fit, a wide toe box, a non-slip sole, and are made from breathable materials. Consult with a podiatrist or diabetes specialist for recommendations on specific shoe brands.
