1. Kiton

Kiton is a luxury Italian brand that has been around since 1956. They are known for their bespoke suits, but they also offer some of the most expensive shirts in the world. A Kiton shirt can cost up to $15,000 due to their use of the finest fabrics, such as Egyptian cotton, and their attention to detail. Each shirt is hand-sewn by skilled artisans and takes up to 50 hours to make. I personally own a Kiton shirt, and the quality and feel of the fabric are unbeatable.

2. Brioni

Brioni is another Italian luxury brand that has been around since 1945. They are known for their high-end suits and formalwear, but they also offer some of the most expensive shirts in the world. A Brioni shirt can cost up to $17,000 due to their use of rare fabrics, such as Vicuna wool, and their meticulous attention to detail. Each shirt is made by hand and takes up to 22 hours to create. I have tried on a Brioni shirt before, and the fit and feel of the fabric were exceptional.

The use of rare materials

One of the reasons why these brands are so expensive is due to their use of rare materials. For example, Brioni uses Vicuna wool, which is one of the rarest and most expensive materials in the world. It comes from the Vicuna, a South American animal that produces a limited amount of wool each year. These luxury brands also use rare types of silk, such as Mulberry silk, which is known for its softness and durability.

How to care for your expensive shirt

If you are lucky enough to own an expensive shirt from one of these luxury brands, it is important to take care of it properly. Always read the care instructions carefully and follow them closely. Avoid washing your shirt too often and when you do, use a gentle detergent and cold water. Hang dry your shirt and avoid putting it in the dryer, as this can damage the fabric. Finally, iron your shirt at a low temperature and avoid using starch, as it can weaken the fibers of the fabric.

3. Charvet

Charvet is a French luxury brand that has been around since 1838. They are known for their bespoke shirts, which are made from the finest materials and are tailored to fit each customer perfectly.
