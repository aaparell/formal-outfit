What Is An Indian Gown Called A Beginner s Guide

Written by Daniel Jul 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Indian fashion has taken the world by storm, but with so many different styles and terms, it can be hard to keep up. One common question that arises is, what is an Indian gown called? In this beginner's guide, we'll explore the answer to that question and more.

When it comes to Indian fashion, there are often cultural and historical significance behind each style. However, for those unfamiliar with the terminology and styles, it can be overwhelming to understand. This is where a guide on what is an Indian gown called can come in handy.

The term "gown" can be used to describe a variety of long dresses. In the context of Indian fashion, a gown is typically a long, flowing dress that is fitted at the top and loose at the bottom. The style of Indian gowns can vary greatly depending on the region and occasion.

In summary, an Indian gown is a long, flowing dress that is fitted at the top and loose at the bottom, with variations in style depending on the region and occasion.

Types of Indian Gowns

When it comes to Indian gowns, there are numerous types and styles to choose from. One popular style is the Anarkali gown, which is named after a legendary dancer from the Mughal era. Anarkali gowns are typically made of flowy materials like chiffon or georgette and feature intricate embroidery and embellishments. Another popular style is the Lehenga gown, which features a long skirt and a fitted top, usually worn for weddings and other formal events.

Personally, I've always been drawn to the elegance of the Anarkali gown. I remember wearing one to a family wedding and feeling like a princess. The way the fabric flowed and the intricate details made me feel beautiful and special.

Choosing the Right Indian Gown

When it comes to choosing the right Indian gown, there are a few things to consider. First, think about the occasion. Is it a formal event like a wedding or a more casual event? This will help determine the style and level of embellishment you should look for.

Next, consider your body type. Indian gowns come in a variety of styles and cuts, so it's important to choose one that flatters your body type. For example, if you have a pear-shaped figure, you may want to choose a gown with a flared skirt to balance out your proportions.

Materials Used in Indian Gowns

Indian gowns are typically made of lightweight, flowy materials like chiffon, georgette, and silk. These materials are ideal for the warm climate in India and allow for easy movement. In addition, Indian gowns often feature intricate embroidery, beadwork, and other embellishments.

Caring for Your Indian Gown

When it comes to caring for your Indian gown, it's important to follow the care instructions on the label. Most Indian gowns are delicate and require special care. For example, some may need to be dry cleaned, while others can be hand washed. In addition, it's important to store your Indian gown properly to prevent damage.

Conclusion of What is an Indian Gown Called

Overall, an Indian gown is a beautiful and elegant dress that can be worn for a variety of occasions. Whether you choose an Anarkali gown or a Lehenga gown, there are numerous styles and designs to choose from. By considering the occasion, your body type, and the materials used, you can find the perfect Indian gown for you.

Question and Answer

Q: Can Indian gowns be worn for casual events?

A: Yes, there are many Indian gowns that are designed for more casual events. Look for gowns made of cotton or other lightweight materials, with less embellishment.

Q: Are Indian gowns only worn by women?

A: While Indian gowns are traditionally worn by women, there are also styles designed for men, such as the sherwani.

Q: Can Indian gowns be customized?

A: Yes, many Indian designers offer custom designs for gowns and other traditional clothing.

Q: What is the history behind Indian gowns?

A: Indian gowns have a long history, with styles evolving over time and differing by region. Some of the earliest Indian gowns can be traced back to the Mughal era, which lasted from the 16th to the 19th century.
