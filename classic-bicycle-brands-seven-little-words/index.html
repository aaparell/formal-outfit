Exploring Hetchins, Cinelli, and Colnago

Let's take a closer look at three of the classic bicycle brands seven little words: Hetchins, Cinelli, and Colnago.

Hetchins: Founded in 1923 by Harry Hetchins, Hetchins bicycles are known for their unique "curly" stays, which were designed to provide extra shock absorption. These bikes were popular among racers in the mid-20th century, and are still prized by collectors today.

Cinelli: Founded in 1948 by Cino Cinelli, an Italian former professional cyclist, Cinelli is known for its innovative designs and high-quality components. Colnago: Founded in 1952 by Ernesto Colnago, this iconic Italian brand has produced some of the most legendary racing bicycles of all time. Colnago bikes are known for their elegant lines, exceptional handling, and cutting-edge technology.

De Rosa: Founded in 1953 by Ugo De Rosa, this Italian brand is known for its innovative designs and high-quality materials. De Rosa bikes have been ridden to victory in some of the most prestigious races in the world.

Pinarello: Founded in 1952 by Giovanni Pinarello, this Italian brand is known for its cutting-edge technology and distinctive designs. Pinarello bikes have been ridden by some of the greatest cyclists in history, including Miguel Indurain and Bradley Wiggins.

Merckx: Founded in 1980 by Eddy Merckx, one of the greatest cyclists of all time, this Belgian brand is known for its exceptional performance and attention to detail. Merckx bikes are designed to be ridden fast and hard, and have been ridden to victory in countless races over the years.
