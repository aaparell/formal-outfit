What Colour Does An Indian Bride Wear

Written by Luffy Jul 19, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Indian weddings are known for their grandeur and traditional rituals which are a perfect blend of culture, customs, and emotions. Weddings in India are an extravagant affair that lasts for several days and involves a lot of preparation. One of the most critical aspects of an Indian wedding is the bride's attire, especially the colour of the outfit she'll wear on her big day.

Choosing the right colour for an Indian bride's wedding dress is a crucial decision. It's a decision that can be overwhelming for the bride and her family, as it sets the tone for the entire wedding ceremony. The colour of the dress should not only look beautiful, but it should also be in line with the customs and traditions of the wedding.

The traditional colour for an Indian bride's wedding dress is red. The red colour symbolizes love, commitment, and prosperity in Indian culture. However, with the evolution of fashion and changing trends, Indian brides have started experimenting with other colours as well. Nowadays, brides are seen wearing colours like pink, green, orange, and blue, along with the traditional red colour.

In conclusion, the colour of an Indian bride's wedding dress holds significant importance in Indian culture. While red is the traditional colour, brides can now experiment with various colours to match their personal style and preferences. The colour of the dress should be a reflection of the bride's personality and the traditions of the wedding.

Why is Red the Traditional Colour for an Indian Bride's Wedding Dress?

Red is the traditional and most popular colour for an Indian bride's wedding dress. The colour red is considered auspicious and symbolizes love, passion, and purity in Indian culture. It's believed that wearing a red wedding dress brings good luck and prosperity to the couple's married life.

When I got married, I wore a beautiful red wedding dress, which was passed down to me from my grandmother. It was a symbol of our family's traditions and my grandmother's blessings for my married life. The dress was a beautiful combination of red, gold, and green, which are the traditional colours for a bride's wedding dress.

Other Colours That Indian Brides Wear on Their Wedding Day

While red is the traditional colour for a bride's wedding dress, Indian brides are now experimenting with various other colours. Pink, green, orange, and blue are some of the popular colours that Indian brides wear on their wedding day.

When my sister got married, she wore a beautiful green and gold lehenga for her wedding. The dress was a reflection of her personality and style, and it looked stunning on her. The green colour symbolizes prosperity and new beginnings in Indian culture, which made it a perfect choice for her wedding dress.

The Significance of Other Colours for an Indian Bride's Wedding Dress

Pink is a popular colour for an Indian bride's wedding dress, as it symbolizes love and happiness. It's a perfect choice for brides who want a traditional yet modern look on their wedding day.

Orange is another popular colour for a bride's wedding dress, as it symbolizes purity and spirituality. It's a vibrant colour that looks beautiful on brides who want to make a bold statement on their wedding day.

Blue is a popular colour for a bride's wedding dress, as it symbolizes trust, loyalty, and stability. It's a perfect choice for brides who want to add a touch of elegance and sophistication to their wedding dress.

How to Choose the Right Colour for an Indian Bride's Wedding Dress

Choosing the right colour for an Indian bride's wedding dress can be overwhelming, but it's essential to keep in mind the traditions and customs of the wedding. The colour of the dress should not only look beautiful, but it should also be in line with the bride's personality and style.

It's always a good idea to consult with a fashion designer or a stylist who specializes in Indian bridal wear. They can guide you in choosing the right colour, fabric, and design for your wedding dress, keeping in mind your preferences and the traditions of the wedding.

Question and Answer

Q: Can an Indian bride wear a white wedding dress?

A: No, in Indian culture, white is the colour of mourning and is not considered appropriate for a wedding dress.

Q: Can an Indian bride wear a black wedding dress?

A: No, black is considered an inauspicious colour in Indian culture and is not worn at weddings.

Q: Can an Indian bride wear a multicoloured wedding dress?

A: While Indian brides are now experimenting with various colours, a multicoloured wedding dress is not a popular choice. It's always a good idea to consult with a fashion designer or a stylist before choosing a multicoloured wedding dress.

Q: Can an Indian bride wear a pastel-coloured wedding dress?

A: Yes, pastel colours like pink, peach, and lavender are a popular choice for an Indian bride's wedding dress. These colours look elegant and sophisticated and are perfect for a modern yet traditional look.

Conclusion of What Colour Does an Indian Bride Wear

The colour of an Indian bride's wedding dress holds significant importance in Indian culture. While red is the traditional colour, brides can now experiment with various colours to match their personal style and preferences. The colour of the dress should be a reflection of the bride's personality and the traditions of the wedding. It's always a good idea to consult with a fashion designer or a stylist before choosing the right colour for an Indian bride's wedding dress.
