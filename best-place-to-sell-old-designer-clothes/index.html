The Best Place To Sell Old Designer Clothes

Written by Aprilia Jul 25, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Do you have a closet full of designer clothes that you no longer wear? Are you looking for a way to make some extra cash and declutter your wardrobe at the same time? Selling your old designer clothes can be a great solution, but figuring out the best place to sell them can be a challenge.

When it comes to selling old designer clothes, there are a few pain points to consider. First, you want to make sure you're getting a fair price for your items. Second, you want to find a platform that's easy to use and has a large audience of potential buyers. Finally, you want to make sure that your items are being sold in a safe and secure environment.

The best place to sell old designer clothes depends on your specific needs and preferences. In this article, we'll explore some of the most popular platforms for selling designer clothes and help you determine which one is right for you.

To summarize, the main points to consider when choosing the best place to sell old designer clothes are price, ease of use, and safety. Now, let's dive into some of the most popular platforms for selling designer clothes.

1. Poshmark

Poshmark is a popular app-based platform for buying and selling clothing, including designer items. It's easy to use and has a large user base, making it a great option for selling your old designer clothes. When you sell on Poshmark, you set your own prices and can negotiate with buyers to reach a fair deal. The platform takes a 20% commission on all sales, but you can keep the rest of the profit.

Personally, I've had a lot of success selling my designer clothes on Poshmark. I appreciate the app's user-friendly interface and the fact that I can easily communicate with potential buyers. Plus, the platform offers shipping labels and handles all transactions, making it a safe and secure way to sell my items.

2. The RealReal

The RealReal is a luxury consignment website that specializes in high-end designer items. If you have high-value designer pieces that you're looking to sell, The RealReal may be a great option for you. The platform offers free in-home pickups in certain cities, making it easy to get your items to them. The RealReal takes a commission on all sales, but the exact percentage varies depending on the item.

While I haven't personally used The RealReal to sell my designer clothes, I know many people who have had success with the platform. The site's focus on luxury items means that you may be able to get a higher price for your items than you would on other platforms.

3. eBay

eBay is a well-known platform for buying and selling a variety of items, including clothing. While it may not be specifically geared towards designer items, it can still be a great place to sell your old designer clothes. One benefit of eBay is that you can set your own prices and choose whether or not to accept offers from potential buyers. However, it's important to note that eBay takes a commission on all sales and there is some risk involved in selling to unknown buyers.

I've used eBay to sell some of my designer clothes in the past and have had mixed results. While I appreciate the platform's flexibility, I've had some issues with buyers not following through on their purchases. Overall, eBay can be a good option if you're willing to take on a bit of risk.

4. Depop

Depop is a mobile app that's popular among younger, fashion-forward crowds. It's a great platform for selling unique, trendy pieces, including designer items. Depop allows you to set your own prices and communicate with potential buyers through the app. The platform takes a 10% commission on all sales.

While I haven't personally used Depop to sell my designer clothes, I know many people who have had success with the platform. If you have more trendy, fashion-forward pieces that may not sell as well on other platforms, Depop may be a great fit for you.

Question and Answer

Q: How do I determine the value of my designer clothes?

A: The value of your designer clothes will depend on a variety of factors, including the brand, the condition of the item, and the current market demand. You can research similar items on the platform you plan to sell on to get an idea of what price range to set.

Q: How do I ensure that my items are being sold safely and securely?

A: Make sure to only sell on reputable platforms that have built-in safety and security features. Additionally, always use tracking and shipping confirmation to ensure that your items are delivered and received by the buyer.

Q: Should I sell my designer clothes individually or as a bundle?

A: It depends on your preference and the items you're selling. If you have several lower-value items, it may make more sense to sell them as a bundle. However, if you have high-value items that are in high demand, it may be more profitable to sell them individually.

Q: What should I do if my items don't sell?

A: If your items don't sell after a certain amount of time, you may want to consider lowering your prices or relisting the items with new photos and descriptions. Alternatively, you can donate the items to a charity or consignment shop.

Conclusion of Best Place to Sell Old Designer Clothes

Ultimately, the best place to sell old designer clothes will depend on your specific needs and preferences. Whether you choose to sell on Poshmark, The RealReal, eBay, Depop, or another platform, make sure to set a fair price, use safe and secure shipping methods, and communicate clearly with potential buyers. With a little effort, you can turn your old designer clothes into cash and make room for new additions to your wardrobe.
