How Much Does An Interior Designer Make

Written by Aprilia Aug 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're considering a career in interior design, one of the top questions on your mind is likely how much money you can expect to make. Money isn't everything, but it's certainly an important factor when it comes to job satisfaction and quality of life. In this article, we'll explore the average salary of an interior designer, as well as the factors that can influence your earning potential in this field.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for interior designers in the United States was $56,040 as of May 2020. This means that half of all interior designers earned more than this amount, while the other half earned less. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $31,970 per year, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $97,510 per year.

In addition to your level of experience and education, several other factors can impact your earning potential as an interior designer. Your location can play a big role, as designers in major metropolitan areas tend to earn more than those in smaller towns or rural areas. Your industry can also influence your salary, with designers in specialized fields like healthcare or hospitality design typically earning more than those in general interior design.

The Importance of Networking

One factor that is often overlooked when it comes to earning potential in interior design is the importance of networking. Building relationships with other professionals in your industry can open up opportunities for higher-paying projects and partnerships that can boost your income. When you attend industry events, make connections with other designers, architects, and contractors who may be able to refer clients to you or collaborate with you on projects.

Building a Strong Portfolio

Another key to success in interior design is building a strong portfolio of your work. Your portfolio is your chance to showcase your design skills and demonstrate your ability to create beautiful, functional spaces that meet your clients' needs. The more impressive your portfolio, the more likely you are to attract high-paying clients who are willing to pay top dollar for your services.

Continuing Education and Certification

Continuing education and professional certification can also play a role in your earning potential as an interior designer. Many employers and clients prefer designers who have completed formal education programs or who hold industry certifications like the National Council for Interior Design Qualification (NCIDQ) certification. Continuing education courses can also help you stay up-to-date on the latest trends and techniques in the field, which can make you a more valuable asset to your clients.

Maximizing Your Earning Potential

If you want to maximize your earning potential as an interior designer, it's important to focus on building a strong brand and reputation for yourself in the industry. This means investing in your education and skills, networking with other professionals, and creating a portfolio of your best work. With dedication and hard work, you can achieve success and financial stability in this exciting and rewarding field.

Question and Answer

Q: How much do entry-level interior designers make?

A: Entry-level interior designers typically earn between $31,970 and $44,950 per year.

Q: Do interior designers make more money if they own their own businesses?

A: Yes, interior designers who own their own businesses can potentially earn more money than those who work for someone else. However, running your own business also comes with additional expenses and responsibilities.

Q: What industries pay interior designers the most?

A: Interior designers who specialize in healthcare, hospitality, or commercial design tend to earn the highest salaries.

Q: Can interior designers work part-time?

A: Yes, many interior designers work part-time or on a freelance basis. However, your earning potential may be lower if you're not working full-time.

Conclusion of How Much Does an Interior Designer Make

Interior design can be a lucrative and rewarding career path for those with a passion for creating beautiful and functional spaces. While your earning potential will depend on several factors like your location, experience, and education, there are many opportunities to increase your income by building a strong brand, networking with other professionals, and investing in your skills and education.
