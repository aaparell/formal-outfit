How To Measure Men s Dress Shirt A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jul 27, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of buying dress shirts that don't fit properly? Do you struggle with finding the right size? You're not alone. Many men find it challenging to measure and buy dress shirts that fit well. But fear not, in this post, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to measure men's dress shirt that will make the process a breeze.

One of the most significant pain points for men when buying dress shirts is finding the right size. Men's dress shirts come in various sizes, and each brand may have different sizing charts. As a result, it can be challenging to know which size to choose. This can lead to frustration and wasted time and money.

Measuring a men's dress shirt is a straightforward process. The first thing you need to do is determine your correct size. You can do this by measuring your neck, chest, waist, and arms. Once you have your measurements, you can compare them to the sizing chart of the brand you want to buy from.

In summary, to measure a men's dress shirt, you need to:

- Measure your neck

- Measure your chest

- Measure your waist

- Measure your arms

- Compare your measurements to the brand's sizing chart

Neck Measurement

When measuring the neck, wrap a measuring tape around the base of your neck, where the collar would sit. Make sure the tape is snug but not too tight. Round up your measurement to the nearest half-inch.

For example, if your measurement is 15 1/4 inches, round it up to 15 1/2 inches. This will be your neck size.

Chest Measurement

When measuring the chest, stand up straight and wrap the measuring tape around the widest part of your chest. Make sure the tape is parallel to the ground and not too tight. Round up your measurement to the nearest half-inch.

For example, if your measurement is 42 1/4 inches, round it up to 42 1/2 inches. This will be your chest size.

Waist Measurement

When measuring the waist, wrap the measuring tape around your natural waistline, which is the narrowest part of your torso. Make sure the tape is snug but not too tight. Round up your measurement to the nearest half-inch.

For example, if your measurement is 36 1/4 inches, round it up to 36 1/2 inches. This will be your waist size.

Arm Measurement

When measuring the arms, start at the top of your shoulder and measure down to your wrist bone. Make sure your arm is relaxed at your side. Round up your measurement to the nearest half-inch.

For example, if your measurement is 33 1/4 inches, round it up to 33 1/2 inches. This will be your arm size.

Shoulder Measurement

When measuring the shoulders, start at the top of one shoulder and measure across to the other shoulder. Make sure the tape is straight and not too tight.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I get accurate measurements by myself?

A: Yes, you can get accurate measurements by yourself, but it may be easier and more accurate to have someone help you.

Q: What if my measurements fall between two sizes?

A: In this case, it's best to go with the larger size. You can always have the shirt tailored to fit you perfectly.

Q: How do I know if the shirt fits properly?

A: The shirt should fit comfortably around the neck and chest, without being too tight or too loose. The sleeves should also be the right length, ending at your wrist bone.

Q: Can I use a flexible tape measure?

A: Yes, a flexible tape measure is ideal for measuring dress shirts as it can conform to your body's contours.

Conclusion

Measuring a men's dress shirt is a simple process that can save you a lot of hassle in the long run. By following our step-by-step guide, you can ensure that you get the right size every time. Remember, a well-fitting dress shirt is essential for a sharp and polished look.
