The Groom's Guide to Traditional Indian Wedding Attire

Planning for a wedding can be overwhelming, especially when it comes to selecting the perfect attire for the occasion. For grooms, the decision of what to wear can be particularly stressful as traditional Indian weddings have specific dress codes and customs that must be followed. In this guide, we will explore what the groom wears in a Hindu wedding and provide some helpful tips to make the process smoother.

The groom's attire is a significant aspect of a Hindu wedding, as it symbolizes his commitment to the ceremony and his bride. It is important to choose clothing that reflects tradition and culture, while also flattering the groom's personal style. The choices can be vast, which can cause confusion and indecisiveness.

What Does the Groom Wear in a Hindu Wedding?

The groom typically wears a traditional outfit called a sherwani, which is a long coat that extends to the knees or ankles. The sherwani is usually made of silk or other luxurious fabrics and is adorned with intricate embroidery or embellishments. It is paired with fitted pants called churidar or dhoti, which are similar to leggings, and a scarf or shawl called a dupatta. The groom also wears a turban or a safa, which is a traditional headpiece made of fabric.

Other accessories that complement the groom's attire include a necklace called a mala, a brooch called a kalgi, and shoes called juttis. The mala is made of flowers or beads and is worn around the neck. The kalgi is attached to the turban and is made of precious metals and stones. The juttis are made of leather and are embroidered to match the sherwani.

Personal Experience

As a groom myself, I can attest to the stress of selecting the perfect outfit for my Hindu wedding. I wanted to honor my culture and tradition while also reflecting my personal style. I chose a sherwani made of raw silk with gold embroidery and paired it with cream-colored churidar and a matching dupatta. I accessorized with a pearl mala and a gold kalgi. The process was challenging but ultimately rewarding.

Tips for Choosing the Perfect Attire

1. Research traditional styles and fabrics to understand the options available.

2. Consider the color scheme of the wedding and choose a complementary color for the outfit.

3. Consult with a reputable tailor or designer to ensure a perfect fit.

4. Accessorize with traditional jewelry and headpieces to complete the look.

Understanding the Significance of the Attire

The groom's attire symbolizes his commitment to the ceremony and his bride. It reflects tradition and culture and is an essential part of the wedding ceremony. The sherwani is worn to show respect and honor to the bride's family and the guests in attendance.

How to Choose the Right Color

The color of the sherwani should complement the bride's attire and the wedding's color scheme. It is customary to avoid white or black as these colors represent mourning and death in Indian culture. Bright colors such as red, gold, and maroon are popular choices for the sherwani.

Personal Experience

When choosing the color of my sherwani, I consulted with my bride to ensure we had a complementary color scheme. We chose a cream and gold combination, which complemented her red and gold lehenga. The colors photographed beautifully and added to the overall aesthetic of the wedding.

Question and Answer

Q: Can the groom wear a suit instead of a sherwani?

A: While it is possible to wear a suit, it is not traditional for a Hindu wedding. The sherwani is an essential part of the ceremony and reflects culture and tradition.

Q: How long does it take to create a custom sherwani?

A: It can take several weeks to create a custom sherwani. It is essential to plan ahead and allow enough time for alterations and fittings.

Q: How do I choose the right accessories?

A: The accessories should complement the sherwani and reflect tradition and culture. Consult with a reputable jeweler or designer to ensure a cohesive look.

Q: Can the groom wear a turban instead of a safa?

A: Yes, a turban is a traditional headpiece that can be worn instead of a safa. It is essential to consult with a reputable tailor or designer to ensure a perfect fit.

Conclusion of What Does the Groom Wear in a Hindu Wedding

The groom's attire is an essential part of a Hindu wedding and reflects tradition and culture. It is important to choose clothing that honors the ceremony and the bride while also reflecting personal style. With proper research and planning, the process can be enjoyable and rewarding.
