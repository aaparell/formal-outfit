So, what is the national dress of Nepal? The national dress of Nepal is called the Daura Suruwal for men and Gunyo Cholo for women. The Daura Suruwal comprises a knee-length shirt called the Daura, and trousers called Suruwal. The Gunyo Cholo consists of a blouse called the Gunyo and a long skirt called the Cholo.

In this article, we'll explore the history and significance of Nepal's national dress, the materials used to make it, how it is worn, and how you can incorporate it into your wardrobe.

The History and Significance of Nepal's National Dress

As mentioned earlier, the national dress of Nepal is steeped in history and cultural significance. It has been worn by Nepali people for centuries and is still an important part of Nepali culture today. The Daura Suruwal and Gunyo Cholo are deeply rooted in the country's history and traditions.

Legend has it that the Daura Suruwal was first worn by King Mahendra Bir Bikram Shah Dev of Nepal in the early 1950s. The dress soon gained popularity among Nepali people and was eventually declared the national dress of Nepal in 2017. The Gunyo Cholo, on the other hand, has been worn by Nepali women for centuries and is still a popular choice for formal occasions.

The Materials Used to Make Nepal's National Dress

The Daura Suruwal and Gunyo Cholo are typically made from cotton or silk fabric. The Daura is a full-sleeved shirt with five pleats and eight strings that tie at the side. The Suruwal is a pair of loose trousers that end just above the ankle. The Gunyo is a blouse with long sleeves and a high collar. The Cholo is a long skirt that falls to the ankles.

How to Wear Nepal's National Dress

Wearing the Daura Suruwal and Gunyo Cholo requires some practice and skill. The Daura Suruwal is typically worn with a waistcoat, a Nepali cap called the Dhaka Topi, and leather shoes. The Gunyo Cholo is worn with a dupatta or shawl and traditional jewelry, such as necklaces and bangles.

How to Incorporate Nepal's National Dress into Your Wardrobe

If you're interested in incorporating Nepal's national dress into your wardrobe, there are a few things to keep in mind.
