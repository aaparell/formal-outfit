What is National Dress in UAE?

Are you curious about the traditional attire worn by locals in the United Arab Emirates? Perhaps you're planning a trip to Dubai or Abu Dhabi and wondering what to wear to respect the culture. In this article, we'll explore what is national dress in UAE, its significance, and how you can incorporate it into your wardrobe.

The national dress of UAE is a symbol of the country's heritage and identity. It reflects the values of modesty, respect, and tradition. For men, the traditional attire consists of a long-sleeved shirt (thobe) and a headscarf (ghutra) held in place by a black cord (agal). Women wear a long, loose-fitting dress (abaya) and a headscarf (hijab) to cover their hair and neck.

The thobe is made of lightweight fabric to combat the hot desert climate. It is ankle-length and comes in various colors, although white is the most popular. The ghutra and agal are traditionally worn to protect the face and head from the sun and sand. The abaya, on the other hand, is often black and made of a flowy material to keep women cool.

To summarize, the national dress in UAE is an essential part of the country's culture and heritage. It represents modesty, respect, and tradition, and is worn by both men and women. The attire includes a long-sleeved shirt, headscarf, and black cord for men, and a long, loose-fitting dress and headscarf for women.

What is the Significance of National Dress in UAE?

Wearing the national dress in UAE is more than just a fashion statement. It is a way to honor and preserve the country's cultural identity. The attire represents the values of modesty, respect, and tradition, which are deeply ingrained in Emirati society. By wearing the national dress, locals and visitors alike show their appreciation for the country's rich history and heritage.

Personally, I had the opportunity to wear the national dress during a visit to Abu Dhabi. I was amazed by the intricate details of the thobe and the elegance of the abaya. I felt a sense of connection to the culture and the people, and it was a humbling experience to be a part of it.

How Can You Incorporate National Dress in UAE into Your Wardrobe?

If you're visiting UAE and want to incorporate the national dress into your wardrobe, there are a few options. For men, you can rent a thobe and ghutra from local shops or purchase them as souvenirs. Women can also rent or purchase abayas and hijabs in various styles and colors. It's essential to respect the culture by keeping the attire modest and appropriate for the occasion.

What are the Different Types of National Dress in UAE?

While the thobe and abaya are the most common national dress in UAE, there are variations depending on the region and occasion. For example, the kandura is a type of thobe worn in the northern part of the country and is often made of thicker fabric. The bisht is a long cloak worn over the thobe for formal events or weddings.

How to Care for National Dress in UAE?

If you do decide to purchase or rent national dress in UAE, it's essential to take proper care of it. The attire is delicate and requires special attention when washing and storing. It's best to follow the instructions provided by the vendor or seek advice from a local tailor.

Conclusion of what is National Dress in UAE

The national dress in UAE is more than just clothing. It's a symbol of the country's heritage, values, and identity. By wearing the attire, locals and visitors alike can show their appreciation for the culture and honor the traditions of the Emirati people. Whether you're planning a trip to UAE or simply curious about the attire, we hope this article has given you a better understanding of what is national dress in UAE.
