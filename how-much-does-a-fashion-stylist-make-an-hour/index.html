How Much Does A Fashion Stylist Make An Hour The Ultimate Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 29, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you interested in pursuing a career as a fashion stylist but not sure if it pays well? Or are you already a fashion stylist and wondering if your hourly rate is competitive enough? If you're asking yourself these questions, you're not alone. Many aspiring and established fashion stylists want to know how much they can expect to make per hour.

The answer to the question of how much does a fashion stylist make an hour is not straightforward. It depends on several factors, such as location, experience, education, and industry. However, we'll do our best to give you a ballpark figure based on research and industry standards.

Based on the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), the median hourly wage for fashion stylists in the United States is $22.17. However, this figure can vary widely, with the lowest 10 percent of fashion stylists earning less than $11.31 per hour and the highest 10 percent earning more than $49.83 per hour. The BLS also reports that the top-paying states for fashion stylists are New York, California, Texas, Florida, and New Jersey.

In summary, the hourly rate for fashion stylists can range from minimum wage to nearly $50 per hour, depending on various factors. In the following sections, we'll dive deeper into these factors and how they affect a fashion stylist's earning potential.

Location and Industry

The location and industry of a fashion stylist play a significant role in determining their hourly rate. As mentioned earlier, the top-paying states for fashion stylists are New York, California, Texas, Florida, and New Jersey. This is because these states have a high demand for fashion stylists and a thriving fashion industry. On the other hand, fashion stylists in less populated or fashion-focused states may earn less due to lower demand.

Similarly, the industry a fashion stylist works in can affect their hourly rate. Fashion stylists who work in high-end fashion, such as for luxury brands or celebrities, may earn more than those who work in retail or e-commerce. This is because high-end fashion requires more specialized knowledge and skills, and the clients are willing to pay a premium for the expertise.

Experience and Education

Experience and education also play a role in a fashion stylist's hourly rate. As with most professions, the more experience and education a fashion stylist has, the higher their earning potential. Fashion stylists who have worked with high-profile clients or on major fashion campaigns can command a higher hourly rate due to their reputation and portfolio. Similarly, fashion stylists with a degree or certification in fashion styling may earn more than those without.

How to Increase Your Hourly Rate as a Fashion Stylist

If you're a fashion stylist looking to increase your hourly rate, there are several things you can do. First, focus on building your portfolio and reputation by working with high-profile clients or on major fashion campaigns. This will not only showcase your skills but also increase your demand and earning potential. Second, consider pursuing additional education or certification in fashion styling to demonstrate your expertise and stand out from the competition. Finally, don't be afraid to negotiate your hourly rate with clients or agencies. If you have the skills and experience to back it up, you may be able to command a higher rate.

Personal Experience: How Much I Make as a Fashion Stylist

As a fashion stylist with five years of experience, I currently charge $50 per hour for my services. However, this rate varies depending on the client and project. For example, I may charge more for high-profile clients or major fashion campaigns. I've found that building a strong portfolio and reputation has helped me increase my hourly rate over time.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it possible to make a full-time income as a fashion stylist?

A: Yes, it's possible to make a full-time income as a fashion stylist. However, it may take some time to build your reputation and clientele to the point where you have consistent work. It's also important to note that the demand for fashion stylists can be seasonal, with busy periods around fashion weeks and major events.

Q: Do fashion stylists typically work on an hourly rate or project basis?

A: Fashion stylists can work on either an hourly rate or project basis. Some clients or agencies may prefer to pay a flat fee for a project, while others may prefer an hourly rate. It's important to clarify the payment terms with clients before starting work.

Q: How much do fashion stylists typically charge for a full-day shoot?

A: The hourly rate for fashion stylists on a full-day shoot can vary widely depending on the location, industry, and experience. However, a common rate is between $500-$1000 for a full-day shoot.

Q: Can fashion stylists charge for travel expenses?

A: Yes, fashion stylists can charge for travel expenses, such as transportation, lodging, and meals. It's important to discuss these expenses with clients before accepting a project and to keep receipts for reimbursement.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Fashion Stylist Make an Hour

In conclusion, the hourly rate for fashion stylists can vary widely depending on several factors, such as location, industry, experience, and education. However, the median hourly wage for fashion stylists in the United States is $22.17, with the top-paying states being New York, California, Texas, Florida, and New Jersey. If you're a fashion stylist looking to increase your hourly rate, focus on building your portfolio and reputation, pursuing additional education or certification, and negotiating your rate with clients or agencies. With hard work and dedication, you can make a full-time income as a fashion stylist.
