How To Take Men s Dress Shirt Measurements

Written by Robby Jul 16, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you tired of buying dress shirts that never seem to fit quite right? Perhaps the arms are too short, or the collar is too tight. Taking accurate measurements before purchasing a dress shirt can make all the difference in achieving a comfortable and flattering fit. In this tutorial, we'll cover everything you need to know about how to take men's dress shirt measurements.

Getting the right fit for a dress shirt can be a difficult process, and it's easy to feel overwhelmed. But don't worry, we're here to simplify the process for you. By taking accurate measurements, you can save yourself time and money by avoiding the hassle of returns and exchanges.

What You'll Need

Before we get started, you'll need a few items:

  • A measuring tape
  • A dress shirt
  • A flat surface to measure on

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these simple steps to take accurate measurements for your dress shirt:

1. Collar Size

Measure around your neck where the collar would sit. Make sure to leave enough room to fit a finger between your neck and the measuring tape for comfort. Round up to the nearest half inch.

2. Sleeve Length

With your arm bent at a 90-degree angle, measure from the center of the back of your neck, over the shoulder, and down to your wrist bone. Round up to the nearest half inch.

3. Chest Size

Measure around the fullest part of your chest, making sure to keep the measuring tape parallel to the ground.

4. Waist Size

Measure around your natural waistline, which is typically the narrowest part of your torso.

5. Hips Size

Measure around the fullest part of your hips, making sure to keep the measuring tape parallel to the ground.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

When taking measurements, there are a few common mistakes to avoid:

  • Measuring over bulky clothing, which can add inches to your measurements
  • Not rounding up to the nearest half inch
  • Not measuring at the right points on your body

Question and Answer

Q: How tight should I measure for my collar size?

A: It's important to measure with enough room to fit a finger between your neck and the measuring tape for comfort. Avoid measuring too tight, as this can lead to discomfort when wearing your dress shirt.

Q: Should I measure with my arms at my sides or bent at a 90-degree angle?

A: For the most accurate sleeve length measurement, it's important to measure with your arm bent at a 90-degree angle. This will ensure that your dress shirt sleeves fit properly when your arms are in a natural position.

Q: Can I measure myself for dress shirt measurements, or do I need someone else to help?

A: While it's possible to measure yourself, it may be helpful to have someone else assist you to ensure the most accurate measurements possible.

Q: How do I know if I should choose a slim fit or regular fit dress shirt?

A: This will depend on your individual body type and preferences. If you prefer a more fitted look, a slim fit dress shirt may be the best option. However, if you prefer a looser fit, a regular fit dress shirt may be more comfortable.

Conclusion

By following this step-by-step guide, you'll be able to take accurate measurements for your dress shirt and achieve a comfortable and flattering fit. Remember to avoid common mistakes and round up to the nearest half inch for the most accurate measurements. With these tips in mind, you'll be able to confidently purchase dress shirts that fit perfectly every time.
