Famous Architecture in Latin America: The Ancient Ruins of Machu Picchu

When it comes to famous architecture in Latin America, few buildings are as iconic as the ancient ruins of Machu Picchu. This incredible site, located in Peru, is one of the most visited tourist attractions in the region, and for good reason. Built by the Incas more than 500 years ago, the ruins of Machu Picchu are a testament to the ingenuity and creativity of this ancient civilization.

When I visited Machu Picchu, I was absolutely blown away by the scale and beauty of the site. The ruins are perched high atop a mountain, and the views from the top are simply breathtaking. As I explored the site, I couldn't help but be amazed by the incredible stonework and the intricate design of the buildings. It was clear that the Incas were a people who were truly ahead of their time.

Famous Architecture in Latin America: The Iconic Designs of Oscar Niemeyer

Another famous architect who has had a major impact on the region is Oscar Niemeyer. This Brazilian architect is known for his bold, modernist designs, which can be found all throughout the region. From the striking National Congress building in Brasilia to the sleek Niteroi Contemporary Art Museum, Niemeyer's buildings are a testament to the power of modern design.

When I visited Brasilia, I was immediately struck by the bold, futuristic designs of the buildings. The National Congress building, in particular, was a sight to behold. The building's sweeping curves and bold lines were unlike anything I had ever seen before, and it was clear that Niemeyer was a true master of his craft.
