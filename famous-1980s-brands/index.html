Exploring Famous 1980S Brands A Tutorial

Written by Oliver Nov 03, 2023 Â· 5 min read

The 1980s were a time of big hair, neon colors, and iconic brands. From fashion to technology, the decade was defined by the brands that shaped popular culture. In this tutorial, we will explore some of the famous 1980s brands and the impact they had on society.

For many people, the 1980s were a time of excess and materialism. Brands were a way to show off wealth and status, and consumers were willing to pay top dollar for the latest trends. However, not everyone was able to afford these luxury items, leading to feelings of exclusion and inadequacy.

The target of this tutorial is to provide an overview of famous 1980s brands and their cultural significance. We will delve into the history and legacy of these brands, as well as their impact on society and consumer behavior.

The Rise of Nike: From Running Shoes to Fashion Empire

As a child of the 80s, I remember the first time I saw a pair of Nike Air Jordans. They were the coolest shoes I had ever seen, and I begged my parents to buy me a pair. Nike was one of the most iconic brands of the decade, known for their innovative designs and celebrity endorsements.

Founded in 1964 as Blue Ribbon Sports, Nike was initially a distributor for Japanese shoemaker Onitsuka Tiger. However, in 1971, the company began producing their own line of shoes, including the famous Nike Cortez. In 1984, Nike signed a deal with basketball superstar Michael Jordan, launching the Air Jordan line and solidifying their place in popular culture.

Today, Nike is one of the biggest brands in the world, with a net worth of over $34 billion. Their influence on fashion and culture is undeniable, with everyone from athletes to celebrities to everyday people sporting the iconic swoosh logo.

Coca-Cola: The Classic Beverage That Defined the 1980s

When you think of the 1980s, one of the first things that comes to mind is the iconic Coca-Cola brand. The soda giant was everywhere in the decade, from TV commercials to billboards to music videos.

Founded in 1886, Coca-Cola has been a staple of American culture for over a century. However, it was in the 1980s that the brand truly became a cultural phenomenon. With their "Coke is It" campaign and iconic "Mean Joe Greene" commercial, Coca-Cola cemented their place in popular culture.

Today, Coca-Cola is still one of the most recognizable brands in the world, with a net worth of over $80 billion. Their influence on popular culture is still felt today, with their logo and branding being used in everything from fashion to music to sports.

The Legacy of MTV: How Music Videos Changed the Game

In the 1980s, MTV was the hottest thing in music. The cable channel, which launched in 1981, played music videos 24/7, bringing the hottest acts of the decade into people's living rooms. From Madonna to Michael Jackson to Prince, MTV was the place to go for the latest in music and fashion.

MTV's influence on popular culture cannot be overstated. The channel not only launched the careers of some of the biggest names in music, but also changed the way people consumed and interacted with music. Today, MTV is still a cultural touchstone, with their Video Music Awards being one of the most-watched events of the year.

The Influence of Apple: How Steve Jobs Changed the Game

When Steve Jobs and Steve Wozniak founded Apple in 1976, they had no idea that they would change the world. However, that's exactly what they did. In the 1980s, Apple was at the forefront of the personal computer revolution, with their Macintosh line of computers becoming a cultural icon.

Today, Apple is one of the biggest brands in the world, with a net worth of over $2 trillion. Their influence on technology and culture is undeniable, with the iPhone becoming one of the most popular and ubiquitous devices in history.

Question and Answer

Q: What other famous 1980s brands had a significant impact on culture?

A: Other notable brands from the 1980s include Levi's, Sony, and McDonald's, all of which had a significant impact on popular culture and consumer behavior.

Q: How did famous 1980s brands influence consumer behavior?

A: Famous 1980s brands often set the trends for fashion, technology, and consumer goods. Many people were willing to pay top dollar for these products in order to show off their wealth and status.

Q: What is the legacy of famous 1980s brands?

A: The legacy of famous 1980s brands is still felt today, with many of these brands continuing to be cultural touchstones. The impact they had on popular culture and consumer behavior cannot be overstated.

Q: Why were famous 1980s brands so influential?

A: Famous 1980s brands were influential because they represented the excess and materialism of the decade. They were a way for people to show off their wealth and status, and many consumers were willing to pay top dollar for the latest trends.

Conclusion of Famous 1980s Brands

The 1980s were a time of big hair, neon colors, and iconic brands. From Nike to Coca-Cola to MTV to Apple, these brands defined popular culture and influenced consumer behavior. Today, their legacy is still felt in everything from fashion to technology to music. Whether you lived through the 80s or are just discovering these brands for the first time, their impact on society cannot be denied.
