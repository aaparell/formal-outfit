How Much Does It Cost To Get A Wedding Dress Tailored

Written by Petter Jun 20, 2023 · 5 min read

Every bride wants their wedding dress to fit them like a glove, but alterations can be expensive. It's important to know how much it will cost to have your wedding dress tailored to avoid any surprises. In this article, we will discuss the cost of wedding dress alterations and what factors can affect the price.

The cost of getting a wedding dress tailored varies depending on several factors such as the type of alterations needed, the complexity of the design, the location of the tailor, and the materials used. It's common for brides to need at least some alterations to their wedding dress to achieve the perfect fit. The cost of alterations can add up quickly, but it's important to keep in mind that a well-tailored dress can make all the difference in how you feel on your wedding day.

The cost of getting a wedding dress tailored typically ranges from $200 to $600, with the average cost being around $400. However, this can vary greatly depending on the factors mentioned above. Simple alterations such as hemming or taking in the sides will cost less than more complex alterations such as changing the neckline or adding sleeves. The cost can also be higher if the dress is made of delicate or expensive materials.

In summary, the cost of getting a wedding dress tailored can vary greatly depending on the type of alterations needed, the complexity of the design, the location of the tailor, and the materials used. It's important to budget for alterations when purchasing your wedding dress to avoid any surprises later on.

Factors That Affect the Cost of Wedding Dress Alterations

When I got married, I had to get my wedding dress tailored to fit me perfectly. The cost of alterations was something that I hadn't considered when I purchased my dress, and it ended up being more expensive than I had anticipated. Here are some factors that can affect the cost of wedding dress alterations:

Type of Alterations Needed

The type of alterations needed will greatly affect the cost. Simple alterations such as hemming or taking in the sides will cost less than more complex alterations such as changing the neckline or adding sleeves. The more complex the alteration, the more expensive it will be.

Complexity of the Design

The complexity of the design will also affect the cost. A simple dress with few embellishments will be easier to alter than a dress with intricate beading or lace. The more complex the design, the more time and skill it will take to alter the dress, which will increase the cost.

Location of the Tailor

The location of the tailor can also affect the cost. Tailors in big cities tend to charge more than tailors in smaller towns. If you live in a big city, you may want to consider traveling to a smaller town to get your alterations done.

Materials Used

The materials used to make the dress can also affect the cost. Delicate fabrics such as silk or lace require more care and skill to work with, which can increase the cost of alterations. Additionally, if the dress has multiple layers or a train, it will take more time to alter, which will also increase the cost.

How to Save Money on Wedding Dress Alterations

While wedding dress alterations can be expensive, there are ways to save money. Here are some tips:

Buy a Dress That Fits Well

The best way to save money on alterations is to buy a dress that fits well. This means trying on dresses in your size and choosing one that requires minimal alterations. You can also consider buying a dress off the rack instead of ordering a custom size.

Choose Simple Alterations

Choosing simple alterations such as hemming or taking in the sides will cost less than more complex alterations. If you love a dress with a complicated design, try to find a similar dress with a simpler design that will be easier and cheaper to alter.

Question and Answer

Q: How much does it cost to hem a wedding dress?

A: The cost to hem a wedding dress varies depending on the complexity of the hem and the tailor you choose. On average, hemming a wedding dress can cost between $100 and $400.

Q: How much does it cost to add sleeves to a wedding dress?

A: Adding sleeves to a wedding dress can cost anywhere from $200 to $800 or more depending on the type of sleeves and the complexity of the design.

Q: How much does it cost to alter a wedding dress neckline?

A: Altering a wedding dress neckline can cost between $150 and $600 or more depending on the complexity of the design and the materials used.

Q: How long does it take to get a wedding dress tailored?

A: The time it takes to get a wedding dress tailored varies depending on the type of alterations needed and the tailor you choose. On average, it can take anywhere from a few days to a few weeks.

Conclusion of How Much Does It Cost to Get a Wedding Dress Tailored

Getting a wedding dress tailored can be costly, but it's important to remember that a well-tailored dress can make all the difference on your wedding day. By understanding the factors that affect the cost of alterations and following the tips to save money, you can ensure that you get the perfect fit without breaking the bank.
