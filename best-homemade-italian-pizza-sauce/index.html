The Best Homemade Italian Pizza Sauce A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Jul 21, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of store-bought pizza sauce that lacks flavor and freshness? Do you want to make your Italian pizza stand out with a homemade sauce that is delicious and easy to make? Look no further! In this tutorial, we will guide you through making the best homemade Italian pizza sauce that will leave your taste buds wanting more.

When it comes to making pizza, the sauce is just as important as the crust and toppings. A good sauce should be flavorful, balanced, and complement the other ingredients on the pizza. Unfortunately, finding a store-bought sauce that meets these requirements can be challenging. That's why making your homemade Italian pizza sauce is the best way to ensure that you get the perfect flavor and consistency every time.

The goal of this tutorial is to provide you with a comprehensive guide to making the best homemade Italian pizza sauce. We will cover everything from the ingredients you need, to the step-by-step process of making the sauce, to tips and tricks for perfecting your sauce.

Ingredients for the Best Homemade Italian Pizza Sauce

Before we get started, let's go over the ingredients you will need for the best homemade Italian pizza sauce. You can adjust the measurements to your liking based on your preferences.

The ingredients you will need are:

  • 1 can (28 ounces) of whole peeled tomatoes
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon of dried basil
  • 1/4 teaspoon of red pepper flakes (optional)

Make sure to use high-quality ingredients to get the best flavor possible.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making the Best Homemade Italian Pizza Sauce

Now that you have all the ingredients, let's get started with the step-by-step process of making the best homemade Italian pizza sauce.

Step 1: Prep the Tomatoes

Start by draining the tomatoes of their juice. You can do this by using a strainer or your hands. Once the juice is drained, crush the tomatoes with your hands or a potato masher until they are in small pieces.

Step 2: Cook the Garlic

Heat the olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and cook until it becomes fragrant, which should take about 30 seconds to 1 minute. Be careful not to burn the garlic.

Step 3: Add the Tomatoes and Seasonings

Add the crushed tomatoes to the saucepan with the garlic and stir to combine. Add the salt, sugar, oregano, basil, and red pepper flakes (if using) to the saucepan and stir until everything is well combined.

Step 4: Simmer the Sauce

Bring the sauce to a simmer and let it cook for about 15-20 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent the sauce from burning. The longer you cook the sauce, the more concentrated the flavor will become.

Step 5: Blend the Sauce (Optional)

If you prefer a smoother sauce, you can use an immersion blender or transfer the sauce to a blender and blend until it reaches your desired consistency. Be careful not to over-blend the sauce, as it can become too thin.

Step 6: Use the Sauce

Your homemade Italian pizza sauce is now ready to use! Spread it on your pizza dough and add your favorite toppings.

Tips and Tricks for Perfecting Your Homemade Italian Pizza Sauce

Here are some tips and tricks to help you perfect your homemade Italian pizza sauce:

  • Use high-quality ingredients for the best flavor.
  • Adjust the seasonings to your liking. Taste the sauce as you go and add more salt, sugar, or spices as needed.
  • Cook the sauce for longer if you prefer a more concentrated flavor.
  • Store the sauce in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a week, or freeze it for up to three months.

Frequently Asked Questions about Homemade Italian Pizza Sauce

Q: Can I use fresh tomatoes instead of canned tomatoes?

A: Yes, you can use fresh tomatoes, but you will need to blanch and peel them first. You will also need to adjust the cooking time, as fresh tomatoes have more water content than canned tomatoes.

Q: Can I make the sauce ahead of time?

A: Yes, you can make the sauce ahead of time and store it in the refrigerator or freezer until you're ready to use it.

Q: Can I use dried garlic instead of fresh garlic?

A: Fresh garlic will give you the best flavor, but you can use dried garlic if you don't have fresh garlic on hand. Use 1/2 teaspoon of dried garlic for every clove of fresh garlic.

Q: Can I use this sauce for other dishes besides pizza?

A: Yes, you can use this sauce as a base for pasta dishes or as a dipping sauce for breadsticks.

Conclusion of Best Homemade Italian Pizza Sauce

Making the best homemade Italian pizza sauce is easy and fun, and it will take your homemade pizza to the next level. With the right ingredients and a little bit of patience, you can create a sauce that is fresh, flavorful, and perfect for your pizza. So what are you waiting for? Get started today!
