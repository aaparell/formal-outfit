Discovering Vintage Designer Sunglasses A Guide To Relive The Glamour And Style Of The Past

Written by Bardi Sep 16, 2023 · 5 min read

Do you ever find yourself wishing for the glamour and style of years gone by? Maybe you’ve watched old movies and wondered how you could capture that same vibe in your own wardrobe. If so, vintage designer sunglasses may be just the thing you need. These timeless accessories can add a touch of elegance and sophistication to any outfit, but finding the right pair can be a challenge.

When it comes to vintage designer sunglasses, there are a few pain points to consider. For one, they’re often hard to find in good condition. Additionally, because they’re designer items, they can be quite expensive. And finally, since they’re vintage, they may not be as sturdy or reliable as modern sunglasses.

However, if you’re willing to put in the effort, vintage designer sunglasses can be a great addition to your wardrobe. Not only do they offer a touch of history and elegance, but they’re also a sustainable option for fashion lovers who want to reduce their environmental impact.

So, how do you go about finding the perfect pair of vintage designer sunglasses? We’ve put together a guide to help you navigate the world of vintage eyewear.

The Target of Vintage Designer Sunglasses

Before we dive into the specifics of finding the perfect pair of vintage designer sunglasses, it’s worth considering what exactly we mean by “vintage” and “designer.” When we say “vintage,” we’re typically referring to items that are at least 20-30 years old. “Designer” refers to sunglasses created by well-known fashion houses or designers, such as Dior, Gucci, or Ray-Ban.

When you combine these two elements – vintage and designer – you get a unique product that offers both history and style. Vintage designer sunglasses can be a way to relive the glamour of the past while also showing off your fashion sense.

Personally, I’ve always been drawn to vintage designer sunglasses. There’s something about the quality and craftsmanship of these items that you just don’t see in modern eyewear. Plus, I love the idea of wearing something with a story behind it – an accessory that has been cherished and cared for by someone else in the past.

How to Find Vintage Designer Sunglasses

If you’re looking to add a pair of vintage designer sunglasses to your collection, there are a few places to start your search. Here are some tips:

1. Thrift stores and vintage shops: These are great places to find vintage items of all kinds, including sunglasses. Keep an eye out for well-known designer brands, and be sure to inspect the sunglasses closely for any damage.

2. Online marketplaces: Websites like Etsy and eBay can be great resources for finding vintage designer sunglasses. Just be sure to read the seller’s reviews carefully and ask any questions you may have before making a purchase.

3. Estate sales and auctions: These can be a bit harder to find, but they can offer some truly unique finds. Look for auctions that specialize in vintage or designer items, and be prepared to bid competitively.

What to Look for When Buying Vintage Designer Sunglasses

When you’re shopping for vintage designer sunglasses, there are a few things to keep in mind:

1. Condition: Look for sunglasses that are in good condition, with no major scratches or damage. Minor wear and tear is to be expected, but be sure to inspect the sunglasses closely before purchasing.

2. Authenticity: Make sure the sunglasses are authentic – there are a lot of knock-offs out there. Look for branding or logos on the sunglasses, and do your research on the specific brand and style to make sure they’re the real deal.

3. Fit: Finally, make sure the sunglasses fit your face properly. Vintage sunglasses can have different shapes and sizes than modern ones, so it’s important to try them on before purchasing.

Caring for Vintage Designer Sunglasses

Once you’ve found the perfect pair of vintage designer sunglasses, it’s important to take care of them properly. Here are a few tips:

1. Store them in a case: When you’re not wearing your sunglasses, be sure to store them in a protective case to prevent scratches and damage.

2. Keep them clean: Use a soft cloth to clean your sunglasses regularly, and be sure to avoid harsh chemicals or abrasive materials.

3. Handle with care: Vintage sunglasses may be more fragile than modern ones, so be sure to handle them with care. Avoid bending or twisting the frames, and be gentle when putting them on or taking them off.

Question and Answer

Q: Are vintage designer sunglasses more expensive than modern ones?

A: It depends on the specific brand and style, but in general, vintage designer sunglasses can be more expensive than modern ones due to their rarity and quality.

Q: Can I wear vintage designer sunglasses with any outfit?

A: Absolutely! Vintage designer sunglasses can add a touch of elegance and sophistication to any outfit.

Q: Can I find vintage designer sunglasses in good condition?

A: Yes, it’s possible to find vintage designer sunglasses in good condition, but it may take some effort and careful searching.

Q: What should I do if my vintage designer sunglasses break?

A: Depending on the extent of the damage, you may be able to have them repaired by a professional. Alternatively, you may need to look for a replacement pair.

Conclusion of Vintage Designer Sunglasses

Vintage designer sunglasses offer a unique combination of history and style. While they may require some effort to find and care for, they can be a sustainable and elegant addition to any wardrobe. Whether you’re looking for a touch of old Hollywood glamour or just want to stand out from the crowd, vintage designer sunglasses are a great option to consider.
