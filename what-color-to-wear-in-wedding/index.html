What Color To Wear In Wedding A Guide To Help You Choose

Written by Luffy Jun 22, 2023 · 4 min read

Weddings are a joyous occasion, but they can also be stressful when it comes to choosing the right outfit. One of the most common questions that comes up is what color to wear in wedding. It can be difficult to know what colors are appropriate and which ones to avoid.

Choosing the right color to wear in a wedding can be a pain point for many people. You want to look your best, but you also don't want to take attention away from the bride and groom. Additionally, there are often unspoken rules about what colors are acceptable to wear, depending on the time of day, season, and location of the wedding.

The answer to what color to wear in wedding ultimately depends on a few key factors. First, consider the time of day that the wedding will take place. For daytime weddings, lighter colors are generally more appropriate, while darker colors are better suited for evening weddings. It's also important to consider the season and location of the wedding. For example, pastel colors are perfect for a spring wedding, while rich jewel tones are ideal for a fall wedding.

In summary, when deciding what color to wear in wedding, it's important to consider the time of day, season, and location of the wedding. Lighter colors are generally better for daytime weddings, while darker colors are more suitable for evening weddings. Pastel colors are perfect for spring weddings, while rich jewel tones are ideal for fall weddings.

Choosing Colors for a Beach Wedding

When we attended my cousin's beach wedding, I had a tough time deciding what color to wear. I did not want to go for a bright color as it would have looked odd with the beach background. Thus, I went for a light blue dress, which was perfect for the occasion. For a beach wedding, it's best to stick to light, airy colors like pastels or soft neutrals. Avoid black or dark colors, and opt for fabrics that are light and flowy.

Choosing Colors for a Winter Wedding

When my friend got married in December, I had a hard time finding the right outfit for the occasion. I wanted to wear something warm but also stylish. After doing some research, I discovered that rich jewel tones like emerald, ruby, and sapphire are perfect for winter weddings. I ended up wearing a deep purple dress, which was both stylish and appropriate for the occasion.

Choosing Colors for a Formal Wedding

For a formal wedding, it's important to choose colors that are elegant and sophisticated. Stick to classic colors like black, navy, and gray, or opt for muted tones like blush or champagne. Avoid bright colors or bold prints, as they can be too loud for a formal occasion.

Choosing Colors for a Destination Wedding

Destination weddings are becoming increasingly popular, but they can also be tricky when it comes to choosing the right outfit. For a destination wedding, consider the location and culture of the wedding. If it's a tropical location, opt for bright colors and bold prints. If it's a more traditional location, stick to classic colors like navy or black.

Question and Answer

Q: Can you wear white to a wedding?

A: It's generally considered inappropriate to wear white to a wedding, as it's the color traditionally worn by the bride.

Q: Can you wear black to a wedding?

A: While black is a classic color, it's not always appropriate for a wedding. It's best to avoid wearing all black, and instead, incorporate some color into your outfit.

Q: Can you wear red to a wedding?

A: Red is a bold color that can be appropriate for a wedding, but it's important to choose the right shade. Avoid bright, fire engine red, and instead, opt for a more subdued shade like burgundy or wine.

Q: Can you wear jeans to a wedding?

A: Jeans are generally not appropriate for a wedding, unless the wedding is very casual and specifically calls for casual attire.

Conclusion of What Color to Wear in Wedding

Choosing the right color to wear in wedding is an important decision, as you want to look your best while also being appropriate for the occasion. By considering the time of day, season, and location of the wedding, you can narrow down your color choices and make an informed decision. Remember to always avoid white, and consider the formality of the wedding when choosing your outfit. With these tips in mind, you'll be sure to choose the perfect color for any wedding you attend.
