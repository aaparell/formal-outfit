What Colour Saree Does An Indian Bride Wear

Written by Christine Aug 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Weddings are a grand affair in India, with vibrant colours, music, and a lot of festivities. A significant aspect of an Indian bride's wedding is her attire, especially her saree. Choosing the right saree is crucial, and the colour of the saree plays an essential role in the bride's overall look.

It can be a daunting task to choose the perfect saree colour for your wedding day, as there are so many options available. However, it is essential to keep in mind the traditions and cultural significance associated with the colours.

Target of What Colour Saree Does an Indian Bride Wear

Indian brides usually wear red or shades of red for their wedding saree. However, there are other colours that brides opt for depending on the region or community they belong to. In this blog post, we will discuss what colour saree an Indian bride wears and the cultural significance behind it.

Red - The Traditional Colour for Indian Brides

Red is the most popular colour for an Indian bride's saree as it symbolizes purity, prosperity, and fertility. It is believed that red brings good luck and wards off evil spirits. In Hindu weddings, the bride wears a red saree, which is also known as 'Sindoori' or 'Shakha Pola'. The red saree is usually paired with gold jewellery, which adds to the bride's overall look.

However, in some regions, brides opt for different shades of red, such as maroon, burgundy, and wine. These colours are also considered auspicious and bring good fortune.

Other Colours for Indian Bridal Sarees

While red is the most common colour for an Indian bride's saree, there are other colours that brides opt for depending on their cultural and regional preferences. Some of the colours are:

White

In some regions of India, the bride wears a white saree instead of a red saree. White symbolizes purity, innocence, and virginity. In Christian weddings, the bride wears a white saree or a gown.

Pink

Pink is another popular colour for an Indian bride's saree. It symbolizes love, happiness, and new beginnings. Lighter shades of pink are perfect for day weddings, while darker shades of pink are suitable for evening weddings.

Green

In Muslim weddings, the bride wears a green saree as it is considered a holy colour. Green symbolizes prosperity, fertility, and harmony.

Gold

Gold is another popular colour for an Indian bride's saree. It symbolizes wealth, prosperity, and success. In some regions, brides wear a gold saree with red borders or motifs.

Personal Experience

As an Indian bride myself, I opted for a red saree with gold embroidery for my wedding day. Red has always been my favourite colour, and I wanted to wear a saree that symbolizes love, prosperity, and happiness. The saree was paired with heavy gold jewellery, which added to my overall look.

Conclusion

Choosing the right colour for an Indian bride's saree is essential as it symbolizes cultural and traditional values. While red is the most common colour for an Indian bride's saree, there are other colours that brides opt for depending on their cultural and regional preferences. It is essential to keep in mind the significance of the colours while choosing the perfect saree for your wedding day.
