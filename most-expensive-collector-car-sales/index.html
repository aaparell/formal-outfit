The Top 5 Most Expensive Collector Car Sales In History

Written by Aprilia Aug 22, 2023 · 5 min read

Have you ever wondered what it would be like to own a piece of automotive history? For car enthusiasts, owning a rare and valuable collector car is the ultimate dream. But what does it take to own one of the most expensive collector cars in the world? In this article, we'll explore the top 5 most expensive collector car sales in history and what makes these cars so special.

What are Most Expensive Collector Car Sales?

Collector cars are more than just vehicles - they are works of art and pieces of history. These cars are coveted by collectors for their rarity, unique features, and historical significance. Most expensive collector car sales refer to the highest recorded sales prices for these rare and valuable cars. These sales represent a small, elite group of cars that are among the most valuable assets in the world.

Now, let's take a look at the top 5 most expensive collector car sales in history:

1. 1962 Ferrari 250 GTO - $48.4 million

The 1962 Ferrari 250 GTO is widely considered to be the most valuable car in the world. Only 39 of these cars were ever made, making them incredibly rare and sought after by collectors. This particular car was owned by former Microsoft executive Greg Whitten and was sold in a private sale in 2018 for a record-breaking $48.4 million.

2. 1957 Ferrari 335 Sport Scaglietti - $35.7 million

The 1957 Ferrari 335 Sport Scaglietti was one of only four ever made and was raced by the likes of Sir Stirling Moss and Mike Hawthorn. This car was sold at auction in Paris in 2016 for a staggering $35.7 million, making it the second most expensive collector car sale in history.

3. 1956 Aston Martin DBR1 - $22.6 million

The 1956 Aston Martin DBR1 was a racing car that won the 1959 24 Hours of Le Mans. It was also driven by racing legends such as Carroll Shelby and Stirling Moss. This car was sold at auction in Monterey, California, in 2017 for $22.6 million, making it the most expensive British car ever sold.

4. 1964 Ferrari 275 GTB/C Speciale Scaglietti - $26.4 million

The 1964 Ferrari 275 GTB/C Speciale Scaglietti is one of only three ever made and was built specifically for racing. This car was sold at auction in Monterey, California, in 2014 for $26.4 million, making it one of the most expensive Ferraris ever sold.

5. 1954 Mercedes-Benz W196 - $29.6 million

The 1954 Mercedes-Benz W196 was a Formula One racing car that was driven by racing legend Juan Manuel Fangio. This car was sold at auction in England in 2013 for $29.6 million, making it the most expensive Formula One car ever sold.

Why are these cars so valuable?

These cars are valuable for a number of reasons. First and foremost, they are incredibly rare - often only a handful were ever made. They are also typically associated with important moments in automotive history or were owned and driven by famous individuals. In addition to their rarity and historical significance, these cars are often incredibly well-preserved and maintained, making them even more valuable.

How do you become a collector car owner?

If you're interested in owning a collector car, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, it's important to do your research and learn as much as you can about the cars you're interested in. This will help you make an informed decision when it comes time to purchase. It's also important to work with a reputable dealer or auction house to ensure that you're getting a legitimate, high-quality car. Finally, be prepared to spend a significant amount of money - collector cars are not cheap!

Conclusion of most expensive collector car sales

Owning a collector car is the ultimate dream for many car enthusiasts. The most expensive collector car sales represent the pinnacle of automotive history and are coveted by collectors around the world. Whether you're a collector or simply an admirer, these cars are a testament to the beauty and engineering excellence of the automotive world.

Question and Answer

Q: What makes a car a collector car?

A: Collector cars are typically rare, unique, or historically significant. They may have been owned by famous individuals or associated with important moments in automotive history.

Q: What should I look for when buying a collector car?

A: When buying a collector car, it's important to do your research and work with a reputable dealer or auction house. Look for a car that is well-preserved, has a documented history, and is in good condition.

Q: How much do collector cars cost?

A: Collector cars can range in price from tens of thousands of dollars to millions of dollars. The most expensive collector cars in the world have sold for tens of millions of dollars.

Q: Are collector cars a good investment?

A: Collector cars can be a good investment, but it's important to do your research and purchase a car that is likely to appreciate in value. It's also important to keep in mind that collector cars require regular maintenance and may not be suitable for everyday use.
