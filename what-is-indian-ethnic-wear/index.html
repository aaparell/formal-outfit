What Is Indian Ethnic Wear

Written by Eveline Jul 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever seen someone wearing a beautiful, colorful outfit and wondered what it's called and where it comes from? If so, you're not alone. Many people are unfamiliar with Indian ethnic wear and may not know what it is or how to wear it.

People often find it challenging to decide what to wear to a special occasion, wedding, or festival. They want to look good, but they also want to feel comfortable. In such situations, Indian ethnic wear can be a perfect choice.

Indian ethnic wear refers to clothing that is traditionally worn by people in India. It includes a wide range of styles, fabrics, and colors, each with its unique history and significance.

So, what are the different types of Indian ethnic wear? Sarees, salwar kameez, lehenga choli, and kurta pajama are some of the most popular styles. These outfits are made from fabrics like silk, cotton, chiffon, and georgette and are adorned with intricate embroidery, beadwork, and embellishments.

Types of Indian Ethnic Wear

One of the most popular types of Indian ethnic wear is the saree. As a child, I remember watching my mother drape herself in a beautiful saree for special occasions. Sarees are long pieces of fabric that are draped around the body and come in many different styles, colors, and fabrics. They are usually worn with a blouse and petticoat.

Another popular style is the salwar kameez, which consists of a long tunic called a kameez, paired with loose-fitting pants called salwar. This outfit is comfortable and versatile, making it perfect for everyday wear.

Lehenga choli is a three-piece outfit that includes a long skirt, a blouse, and a dupatta (scarf). It's a popular choice for weddings and festivals and is often adorned with intricate embroidery and beadwork.

Choosing the Right Indian Ethnic Wear

When choosing Indian ethnic wear, it's essential to consider the occasion, the weather, and your personal style. If you're attending a wedding, you might want to opt for a more formal outfit like a saree or lehenga choli. For everyday wear, a salwar kameez or kurta pajama might be more appropriate.

It's also essential to consider the weather. If you're attending an outdoor event in the summer, you might want to choose a lightweight fabric like cotton or chiffon. In the winter, silk or velvet might be a better choice.

How to Accessorize Indian Ethnic Wear

When it comes to accessorizing Indian ethnic wear, the possibilities are endless. Jewelry is a crucial part of any Indian outfit and can include earrings, necklaces, bangles, and bracelets.

If you're wearing a saree, a statement necklace or earrings can add a touch of elegance to your outfit. For a salwar kameez or kurta pajama, a pair of jhumkas (traditional Indian earrings) and a bangle or two might be all you need.

Conclusion of What Is Indian Ethnic Wear

Indian ethnic wear is a beautiful and vibrant representation of India's rich culture and history. It includes a wide range of styles, fabrics, and colors, each with its unique significance. When choosing Indian ethnic wear, it's essential to consider the occasion, weather, and personal style. Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and accessories to find what works best for you.
