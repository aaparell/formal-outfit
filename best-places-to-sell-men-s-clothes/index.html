Best Places To Sell Men s Clothes

Written by Robby May 17, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you looking for the best places to sell your gently used men's clothes? It can be frustrating to have a closet full of clothes that you no longer wear, but don't know what to do with. Fortunately, there are many options available for selling your clothes and making some extra cash. In this article, we will explore the best places to sell men's clothes to help you find the perfect solution for your needs.

When it comes to selling men's clothes, there are many factors to consider, such as the condition of the items, the brand, the style, and the season. You may also have personal preferences for the selling process, such as convenience, speed, or profitability. Depending on your priorities, you may choose different platforms or methods to sell your clothes. Some options may be more suitable for high-end designer pieces, while others may be better for casual everyday wear. Some options may require more effort and time from you, while others may be more hands-off.

So, what are the best places to sell men's clothes? Here are some of the top options:

1. Online Marketplaces

Online marketplaces such as eBay, Amazon, and Etsy are popular choices for selling men's clothes. These platforms allow you to create your own listings and set your own prices. They also offer a wide audience of potential buyers from all over the world. However, you will need to handle the shipping and handling yourself, and may have to pay fees and commissions to the platform. You may also face competition from other sellers, and may need to invest time and effort into creating attractive listings and responding to customer inquiries.

Personally, I have had success selling my vintage clothing on Etsy. I found that the niche audience on the platform was interested in unique and quirky pieces. I also appreciated the ability to customize my shop and brand.

2. Resale Websites

Resale websites such as Poshmark, Grailed, and ThredUp are designed specifically for buying and selling secondhand clothing. These platforms offer a streamlined process for selling your clothes, as they handle the shipping and handling for you. They also have built-in audiences of buyers who are looking for pre-owned clothing. However, they may have stricter guidelines for the condition and authenticity of the items you can sell, and may take a commission or offer a lower payout than other options.

Recently, I have started using Poshmark to sell my gently used clothes. I appreciate the ease of use and the social aspect of the platform, as you can follow other sellers and share your listings with your followers. I have also found that the buyers on Poshmark are more willing to pay full price for items, rather than haggling or low-balling.

3. Local Consignment Shops

Local consignment shops are a great option if you prefer to sell your clothes in person and support small businesses in your community. These shops typically have a curated selection of high-quality items, and may offer a higher payout than online options. They may also provide you with feedback on your items and help you determine the best price. However, they may have limited hours, locations, or demand for certain styles or sizes.

When I lived in a college town, I would sell my trendy clothes at a local consignment shop that catered to the student population. I found that the staff was friendly and knowledgeable, and that the items sold quickly due to the high demand for affordable fashion.

4. Garage Sales

Garage sales may seem old-fashioned, but they can still be a viable option for selling men's clothes. If you have a lot of items to sell and want to get rid of them quickly, a garage sale can be a fun and social way to do so. You can set your own prices and negotiate with buyers in person. However, you may not get as much money as you could from other options, and may have to deal with the hassle of setting up and taking down your sale.

When I was moving to a new city and needed to downsize my wardrobe, I held a garage sale with my roommates. We put up signs around the neighborhood and offered free lemonade to attract shoppers. While we didn't make a ton of money, we had a good time and were able to clear out our unwanted clothes.

Question and Answer

Q: Do I need to have designer clothes to sell?

A: No, you can sell any gently used men's clothes that are in good condition, regardless of the brand or style. However, certain platforms may be more suitable for high-end or luxury items.

Q: How do I determine the price of my items?

A: You can research similar items on the platform you plan to use, or check out other resale websites or local consignment shops to get an idea of the market value. You can also consider the original price, condition, and demand for the item, as well as any fees or commissions you may need to pay.

Q: What should I do with clothes that are not in good condition?

A: If your clothes are torn, stained, or damaged, they may not be suitable for selling. You can try donating them to a local charity or recycling them through a textile recycling program.

Q: How can I maximize my profits?

A: You can try bundling similar items together, offering discounts for bulk purchases, or promoting your listings on social media or other channels. You can also provide clear and detailed descriptions and photos of your items to attract buyers.

Conclusion of Best Places to Sell Men's Clothes

As you can see, there are many options for selling men's clothes, depending on your preferences and priorities. Whether you choose to sell online, in person, or through a combination of methods, you can turn your unwanted clothes into cash and declutter your closet. By considering the condition, brand, style, and season of your items, as well as the fees, commissions, and demand of the selling platform, you can make informed decisions and maximize your profits.
