How To Get Cowboy Boots On Easier

Written by Eveline Oct 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Looking for a new pair of cowboy boots but struggling to get them on? Don't worry, you're not alone. Getting cowboy boots on can be challenging, especially if you're new to wearing them. But with a few tips and tricks, you can make the process much easier and comfortable.

Many people struggle with getting cowboy boots on because of the narrow opening at the top of the boot. This can make it difficult to slide your foot in, and sometimes even painful. Additionally, the stiff leather material of the boots can make them feel tight and uncomfortable. These issues can be frustrating, but there are ways to overcome them.

Invest in a Boot Jack

A boot jack is a handy tool that can make getting cowboy boots on much easier. It works by holding the heel of the boot in place while you slide your foot in, eliminating the need to struggle with the narrow opening. Simply place the heel of the boot in the jack and use your foot to push your heel down while sliding your foot in. This will help to prevent any discomfort or pain, and make the process much smoother.

Wear Thinner Socks

Another tip to make getting cowboy boots on easier is to wear thinner socks. Thick socks can make it difficult to slide your foot into the boot, and can also make the boots feel tight and uncomfortable. Instead, opt for thinner socks that will allow your foot to slide in more easily. This will also help to prevent any discomfort or pain, and make the boots feel more comfortable once they're on.

Use a Boot Horn

If you don't have a boot jack, a boot horn is another useful tool that can help make getting cowboy boots on easier. A boot horn works by sliding into the back of the boot and creating a wider opening for your foot. This can make it much easier to slide your foot in, especially if you're wearing thicker socks or have wider feet. Boot horns are relatively inexpensive and can be found at most shoe stores or online retailers.

Break Them In

One of the most important things you can do to make getting cowboy boots on easier is to break them in. New cowboy boots can be stiff and uncomfortable, making it difficult to get them on and wear them for extended periods of time. To break them in, wear them around the house for short periods of time, gradually increasing the amount of time you wear them each day. This will help to soften the leather and make the boots more comfortable to wear.

Conclusion of How to Get Cowboy Boots On Easier

Getting cowboy boots on can be a frustrating and uncomfortable experience, but it doesn't have to be. By investing in a boot jack or boot horn, wearing thinner socks, and breaking your boots in, you can make the process much easier and more comfortable. These tips will help you get your cowboy boots on with ease, so you can enjoy wearing them all day long.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I use soap to make my boots slide on easier?

A: We do not recommend using soap to make your boots slide on easier. This can cause the leather to dry out and crack, and can also damage the stitching. Instead, invest in a boot jack or boot horn to make the process easier and more comfortable.

Q: How tight should cowboy boots be?

A: Cowboy boots should fit snugly, but not be too tight. They should be comfortable to wear and allow your toes to move freely. If your boots feel too tight or uncomfortable, try wearing them around the house for short periods of time to break them in.

Q: How do I know if my cowboy boots are the right size?

A: Your cowboy boots should fit snugly around the foot and ankle, with no slipping or sliding. You should be able to wiggle your toes freely, and the boots should feel comfortable to wear. If your boots feel too tight or too loose, try a different size or style.

Q: How can I make sure my cowboy boots last a long time?

A: To ensure your cowboy boots last a long time, it's important to take care of them properly. Store them in a dry, cool place when not in use, and clean them regularly with a leather cleaner and conditioner. Avoid wearing them in wet or muddy conditions, and have them resoled when necessary.
